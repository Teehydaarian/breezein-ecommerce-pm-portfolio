## What Each Presentation Contains

### Kick-Off and Planning
- **01 - Project Kick-Off (Wk 1):** Agenda, project background, objectives, scope, team roles, timeline, budget, ways of working
- **02 - Steering Committee 1 (Wk 2):** Phase 1 health check, decisions required, budget snapshot, charter approval

### Sprint 1 - Design Phase
- **03 - Sprint 1 Planning (Wk 3):** Sprint goal, definition of done, full sprint backlog with story points
- **04 - Weekly Status (Wk 4):** RAG status, achievements this week, phase progress, budget tracker
- **05 - Sprint 1 Retrospective (Wk 5):** Results against goal, what went well, what to improve, carried-over items
- **06 - Steering Committee 2 (Wk 5):** End of phase health check, Go/No-Go decision for development

### Sprint 2 - Development Phase
- **07 - Sprint 2 Planning (Wk 6):** Sprint goal, weekly sub-goals, full 12-task development backlog
- **08 - Weekly Status (Wk 7):** Mid-development progress, RAG update, risk and budget tracker
- **09 - Weekly Status (Wk 8):** CMS delay identified and escalated, new risk logged, £800 contingency used
- **10 - Sprint 2 Retrospective (Wk 9):** Midpoint health check, team friction points, action log
- **11 - Steering Committee 3 (Wk 10):** Budget actuals vs forecast, Go/No-Go for QA phase

### Sprint 3 - QA and Testing Phase
- **12 - Sprint 3 Planning (Wk 10):** Testing strategy, five test types defined, QA backlog
- **13 - UAT Readiness Review (Wk 11):** 10-point readiness checklist, UAT scenarios, success criteria
- **14 - Weekly Status (Wk 11):** Bug tracker, QA pass rate, performance metrics
- **15 - Sprint 3 Retrospective (Wk 12):** UAT results (88%), stakeholder feedback, lessons captured
- **16 - Final Steering Committee (Wk 12):** Full KPI status, go-live authorisation requested

### Launch and Close-Out
- **17 - Go-Live Launch Day (Wk 13):** Step-by-step launch checklist, Day 1 metrics, live site performance
- **18 - Post-Launch Review (Wk 14):** Week 1 data, issues log, next 30 days action plan
- **19 - Lessons Learned and Close-Out (Wk 15):** Full delivery scorecard, lessons by category, recommendations

## PM Frameworks Used

- **MoSCoW Prioritisation** - agreeing what gets built and what does not before development starts
- **RAG Status Reporting** - keeping stakeholders informed with simple, honest traffic light updates
- **RAID Log** - tracking risks, assumptions, issues and dependencies across the full project lifecycle
- **Go/No-Go Gate Reviews** - getting formal approval before moving between phases
- **Sprint Planning and Retrospectives** - agile ceremonies to plan work and improve each sprint
- **Stakeholder Engagement Matrix** - understanding who needs what and communicating accordingly
- **Project Charter** - defining scope, governance and success criteria from day one
- **Budget tracking** - monitoring spend vs plan every single week
- **UAT Planning and Facilitation** - running structured user acceptance testing with client stakeholders
